# User and Decision — Plan

*MMA 616 · Stage: PLAN*

## Primary user
A **social-assistance program owner / policy lead** — accountable for where the program directs
its review and planning attention.

## Secondary users
Senior policy leads who consume the briefing; the **AI Council** (human governance body)
that reviews outputs before they are treated as decision-ready.

## The decision supported
> *Which areas and groups should we focus on this cycle?*

i.e., which provinces / regions / age or demographic groups — a geography × demographic
intersection, e.g. *youth in Alberta* — warrant a closer human look because economic distress
(labour-market, income, and employment signals together) is elevated or worsening.

## What the decision is NOT
- **Not** "who needs social assistance."
- **Not** an eligibility, benefit, approval, denial, or reduction decision.
- **Not** the choice or delivery of any intervention (e.g., a training program) — that is the
  owner's downstream call.
- **Not** any individual-level judgment. The system uses **aggregate public data only**.

## How the tool makes the decision faster / better
It ranks areas (geography × group) by a transparent 0–100 triage score, shows the drivers and
the evidence behind each flag, flags missing/weak data, and lets the owner compare across
geography, group, and time — replacing manual cross-tabulation with a governed, source-linked
view.

> **Governance.** This is a *triage* tool: it flags where to prioritise human policy review. It does **not** determine social-assistance need, eligibility, or benefits, and never operates on individuals. Claude outputs are claims, not facts; missing data is flagged, never invented; the AI Council reviews interpretations before deployment.
