# Opportunity Brief — Plan

*MMA 616 · Agentic Project Design · Stage: PLAN*

## The opportunity
A social-assistance program owner / policy lead must triage limited attention across many
provinces, regions, age groups, and demographic groups. Today that prioritisation leans on
scattered tables and manual reading. **A governed dashboard that turns aggregate Statistics
Canada data into a transparent, comparable economic-distress signal — combining labour-market,
income, and employment indicators — lets the owner see which areas and groups are elevated or
worsening and decide where to focus first, with the evidence and caveats attached to every
flag.**

## The project
**Labour Market Stress & Social Support Prioritization Dashboard** — a governed
decision-support tool that flags provinces / regions / age & demographic groups showing
elevated economic distress (labour-market, income, employment) so a **human** can decide which
areas and groups to focus on for policy review. Choosing or delivering any intervention (e.g.,
an employment-training course) is the owner's downstream call — out of scope here.

## Why it is worth building
- Triage attention is scarce; the cost of looking in the wrong place is real.
- The signal is reproducible, explainable, and source-linked — defensible in a briefing.
- It demonstrates the MMA 616 thesis: the difference between **AI capability** (compute a
  score, draft a briefing) and **human judgment** (define good output, test it, govern it).

## What Claude does vs. what humans own
Claude cleans data, computes the triage score, drafts briefing text. Humans (and the AI
Council) define "good," verify every claim, and make every real decision.

> **Governance.** This is a *triage* tool: it flags where to prioritise human policy review. It does **not** determine social-assistance need, eligibility, or benefits, and never operates on individuals. Claude outputs are claims, not facts; missing data is flagged, never invented; the AI Council reviews interpretations before deployment.
