## Why governance
A wrong or overconfident labour-market read can misdirect scarce analyst attention or be
misused to justify a decision the data cannot support. Governance separates **AI capability**
from **human judgment**: Claude computes signals and drafts briefings; the **AI Council**
(human body) decides whether those claims are good enough — and in-scope — to act on. The
single hardest boundary: **triage signal for review, never a determination of who needs
assistance.**

## Approval criteria (all must hold)
Accurate · Useful · Clear · Appropriate (triage not eligibility) · Governed · Safe · In-scope ·
Evidence-based.

## The three decisions
**Approve** · **Approve with revisions** (changes recorded & verified) · **Do not deploy**
(stays prototype, re-reviewed). Nothing is deployed or called "reliable" before a decision.

> **Governance.** This is a *triage* tool: it flags where to prioritise human policy review. It does **not** determine social-assistance need, eligibility, or benefits, and never operates on individuals. Claude outputs are claims, not facts; missing data is flagged, never invented; the AI Council reviews interpretations before deployment.
