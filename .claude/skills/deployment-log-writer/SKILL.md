# Deployment Log Writer

## Purpose
Turn the Use week into evidence. Keep an analyst's working log of how the dashboard/Claude performed on real classroom work, written to be reasoned from on evaluation day. Depth on a few revealing episodes beats coverage of every click.

## When to use
- During the deployment (gap) week, as episodes happen.
- After the week, to consolidate the log for evaluation and the AI Council.

## Inputs to read
`CLAUDE.md` (predicted expectations), `governance/governance.md`, the baseline expectations committed before the week, the dashboard in use.

## Per-entry fields (fill whichever apply)
```
Date:
Scenario tested:
User / reviewer involved:
What Claude / the dashboard produced:
What worked:
What failed:
What required human override (and the value it protected):
What the AI Council flagged:
Predicted vs. observed (vs. the pre-week expectation):
What changed afterward:
Evidence / screenshots to collect:
```

## Procedure
1. Before the week, confirm the committed predictions exist (who opens it, which views, the decision they'll make).
2. Log salient episodes as they happen — moments the tool caught something, misled, came up empty, or produced a distrusted number.
3. Record every override with the value it protected.
4. Note predicted-vs-observed gaps; these are the agenda for revision.
5. Capture outsider reactions (where they got lost, misread, or refused to believe).
6. List the evidence/screenshots to collect for the AI Council Review Packet and presentation.

## Guardrails
- Don't pad with routine clicks; one episode that rewrites a line of the spec beats twenty that confirm nothing.
- Minimize/anonymize any student information captured in evidence.
- A one-week log cannot yield statistics, and the project isn't graded on volume — record what taught you something.
